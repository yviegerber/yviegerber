I wished to be a saint but instead became a martyr
for what though, I do not know.

Still, they pay me tribute,
and my allowance pays for the train to the stop at the bottom of the hill.
I lay near the top with my knees bent
the cat always comes and there we lie
my hands above my head, my chest open.

I wear braids to protect myself, but I don’t think it works. They catch my feet as I’m dancing and I fall on the ballroom floor
down falls the iron curtain and there I lay,
blushed cheek on the cool marble, clouded eyes towards the crowd
And they clap
a fallen angel,my molten wings drip molasses,
syrupy liqueur down my spine.
They say that you are the dichotomy of your heaven and hell
and I can confirm this is true, as I lie
in the ballroom of my mind.
